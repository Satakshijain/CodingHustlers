    Open Source

    Before diving into open source, it's important to understand Git and GitHub. These are essential tools that help you establish your presence and contribute effectively to the open source community. There are several beginner-friendly repositories on GitHub that offer a great way to gain hands-on experience on "How one can contribute to open Source projects"

    • Awesome-for-Beginners
    • Homebrew
    • The Algorithm
    • 30-seconds
    • Great for new Contibutors

    Fellowships

    After learning about how you can contribute, it's time to gain practical experience by collaborating with experts. Here are some programs and fellowships that offer valuable experience, a stipend to appreciate your work, and the opportunity to showcase your skills.

    • Outreachy
    • Google Summer of Code
    • Hacktoberfest
    • Google Season of Docs (GSoD)
    • Hyperledger Mentorship Program
